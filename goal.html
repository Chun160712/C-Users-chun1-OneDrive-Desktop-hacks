SMART Studying

S for Specific!

States in clear terms what outcome, result or behaviour is to be achieved.

Questions to consider:
What do I want to accomplish on this goal?
How will I accomplish it?
Why do I want to accomplish is?
Who will need to be involved?

M for measurable!

Includes a description of the desired outcome and/or measurable results.

Questions to consider:
How will I measure this?
How will I know when I’ve completed it?
How will I know if I’m on track to complete this goal?

Enter method of Measuring your goal:

A for Achievable!

A realistic expectation, given time and resources.

Questions to consider:
Do I have the skills and knowledge currently to achieve this goal?
Is this the right time for me to work on this goal?
Do I have enough time and resources to achieve this goal?
Does this goal challenge me in the right way?

Is your goal achievable? Be realistic and honest!>

Great! Continue to R!

Edit your goal to make it more achievable!

R for Relevant!

There is a clear link to the goals of the department, division, the company and its values.

Questions to consider:
Is this worth my time and effort?
How does this link to my future path?
Is this the right time to complete this program for me?

How relavant and significant is your goal?

T for Time-Bound!

There is a time limit or deadline by which the objective must be achieved, and there may be a time frame to track phases of completion in an action plan.

Questions to consider:
When do I want to complete the program by?
How long do I want to allocate to each module?
How will I track my progress against time per module?
What is my target end date?

When is the target date of your goal? (Think of it carefully as you will be breaking it down into smaller tasks and integrating them into your daily routine!)
